Welcome to the \textit{Payment Dictionary for Idiots}, a learning resource designed to demystify the complex terminology of the payment industry.

\section*{Purpose}

This dictionary aims to explain payment industry concepts in plain, accessible language. Whether you're a student, developer, business professional, or simply curious about how payments work, this resource will help you understand the jargon.

\section*{How This Dictionary Grows}

This is a living document. Terms are added incrementally as the payment industry evolves and as we identify concepts that need clearer explanation. Our goal is to build a comprehensive resource covering approximately 100 core payment terms.

\section*{Philosophy}

We believe that understanding payment terminology should not require years of industry experience. Each entry is written with the assumption that you're encountering the term for the first time. We avoid circular definitions and industry jargon in our explanations.

\subsection*{Cross-Domain Analogies}

This dictionary uses an innovative approach: explaining payment concepts through cross-domain analogies, particularly from biological and medical systems. Why? Because payment systems and biological systems share fundamental characteristics---both are real-time, irreversible, high-risk systems where error handling matters more than raw performance.

Just as the immune system uses checkpoints to verify cells, payment systems use authorization gates. Just as neurons route signals through switching centers, payment networks route transactions through switches. These analogies help you understand not just \textit{what} payment mechanisms do, but \textit{why} they exist and \textit{how} they protect the system.

When you see a ``Biological Analogy'' or ``Medical Analogy'' section, it's designed to build intuition by connecting unfamiliar payment concepts to familiar patterns from nature.
