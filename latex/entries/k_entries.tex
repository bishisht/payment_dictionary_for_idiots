\dictentry{Key Management}{/kiː ˈmænɪdʒmənt/, \textit{n.}}{
The entire lifecycle of cryptographic keys, including their generation,
storage, distribution, use, and eventual destruction. In the payment industry,
robust key management (like DUKPT) is critical to ensuring that sensitive data
remains secure even if parts of the network are compromised.

\example{A payment processor's key management policy defines how often they
change their master keys and how they securely transport new keys to payment
terminals.}

\analogy{Biological}{Like \textbf{DNA Replication and Repair}. The biological
system must precisely manage the "keys" (genetic code) that control the cell. It
must ensure the code is copied accurately (distribution), kept safe in the
nucleus (storage), and that any damaged "keys" are repaired or destroyed to
prevent mutations (security compromise).}
