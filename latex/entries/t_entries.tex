\dictentry{Terminal Management System}{/ter-mi-nal man-ij-ment sis-tem/, \textit{n.}}{
Also known as TMS. A centralized software platform that remotely monitors, controls, and updates payment terminals (card readers) used in stores, restaurants, and businesses. The TMS allows payment companies to install software updates, fix problems, change settings, and monitor the health of thousands of payment devices without physically visiting each location. It's like having remote control over all payment terminals from one central dashboard.

\example{When your credit card reader at a coffee shop needs a security update, the payment company uses their Terminal Management System to send the update wirelessly overnight, so the device works normally the next morning without a technician visit.}

\crossref{\textbf{POS}, \textbf{Payment Terminal}, \textbf{EMV}, \textbf{Software Update}}

\etymology{Terminal Management Systems became essential in the 2000s as payment terminals evolved from simple card readers into sophisticated computers requiring regular software updates and security patches.}
}
