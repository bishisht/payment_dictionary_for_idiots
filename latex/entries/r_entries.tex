\dictentry{RFID}{/ˌɑːr ɛf aɪ ˈdiː/, \textit{abbr.}}{
Radio Frequency Identification. A technology that uses electromagnetic fields to
automatically identify and track tags attached to objects. In the payment
industry, RFID is the underlying technology for contactless cards and mobile
wallets, allowing data to be transmitted without physical contact or line-of-
sight.

\example{The tiny chip and antenna embedded in your credit card use RFID to
communicate with the store's terminal when you hold it nearby.}

\analogy{Biological}{Like \textbf{Olfaction} (Sense of Smell). Just as an animal
can identify a specific object or predator by the "signals" (scent molecules)
drifting in the air without having to see or touch it, an RFID reader "smells"
 the unique digital signature of a tag within its range.}

\crossref{\textbf{Contactless}, \textbf{ISO/IEC 14443}, \textbf{ISO/IEC 15693}, \textbf{NFC}}

\etymology{Coined in the mid-20th century; the technology was used as early as
World War II for "Identification Friend or Foe" (IFF) systems for aircraft.}
}
