\dictentry{HSM}{/ˌeɪtʃ ɛs ˈɛm/, \textit{abbr.}}{
Hardware Security Module. A specialized, highly secure physical device used to
safeguard and manage cryptographic keys and perform encryption and decryption.
HSMs are designed to be tamper-resistant; if someone tries to physically open
one, it will immediately delete all its keys to prevent them from being stolen.

\example{Banks use HSMs to store the master keys used by the DUKPT scheme. When
you enter your PIN, the bank's HSM is the only place where the key can be
re-derived to verify your identity.}

\analogy{Biological}{Like a \textbf{Bacterial Endospore}. Just as an endospore
is a tough, dormant, and highly resistant structure that protects a bacterium's
vital genetic material from extreme environmental stress (heat, radiation,
chemicals), an HSM is a "hard shell" that protects a payment system's vital
cryptographic keys from physical or digital attacks.}

\crossref{\textbf{DUKPT}, \textbf{Encryption}, \textbf{Key Management}}

\etymology{Short for Hardware Security Module. These devices became standard in
the banking industry in the late 20th century to meet stringent security
requirements.}
}
