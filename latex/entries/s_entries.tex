\dictentry{Smart Card}{/smɑːrt kɑːrd/, \textit{n.}}{
A physical card that has an embedded integrated circuit (chip) capable of
storing and processing data. Smart cards are widely used in payments (EMV
cards), identification, and transit because the chip provides significantly
better security than traditional magnetic stripes.

\example{Your credit card with a small gold or silver chip on the front is a
smart card.}

\analogy{Biological}{Like a \textbf{Cell Nucleus}. While a magnetic stripe is
like a piece of paper that just stores information, the chip on a smart card is
like the nucleus of a cell—it doesn't just hold the "DNA" (your account data),
it also contains the machinery to perform tasks and verify information
internally.}

\crossref{\textbf{EMV}, \textbf{ISO/IEC 7816}, \textbf{PICC}}
}
